XXIX

The God of Peace shall bruise Satan under your feet shortly.

Harry Trueman was grateful that the Child had come to him in this last hour of pain.

“I told you once⁠—far away from here⁠—that the day would come when the World and the Church would lie at your feet, and that then you would know Darkness and Light!”

“And you told me,” breathed the young soldier, “to fling down the weapons of Darkness and take up the weapons of Light!”

“Yes⁠—you did not understand then⁠—but now you know!”

“Yes, now I know!” he answered more firmly.

“And tonight,” said the Child, “the Message will go forth to all the world: “How long halt ye between two thoughts? If the LORD be God, follow Him; but if Baal, then follow him!”

“I have already chosen, beautiful Child!” Yet the soldier’s brow was damp with the sweat of agony. “Can you not understand that in all good faith I took up my sword to die for Him, yet in His hour of need I denied Him?”

“He knows even that!” said the Child softly, “and you shall not, even yet, lack suffering for His sake.”

The Child was gone. The man turned off the lights, and went out for half an hour into the darkened streets.
